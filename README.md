# Kia & Hyundai Vehicle Theft Analysis: Visualizing a National Theft Trend

## Overview

This project examines the dramatic rise in Kia and Hyundai vehicle thefts across the United States between 2019 and 2023. Using publicly available theft data, the project explores geographic trends, city-level impacts, regional differences, and the disproportionate increase in thefts involving Kia and Hyundai vehicles.

The primary focus of this project is data visualization and public awareness. Through infographics and supporting visual analyses, complex theft data is transformed into clear and accessible visual stories that help audiences understand the scope of the issue and its impact on communities across the country.

The project was developed as part of a data visualization assignment emphasizing visual communication, audience-focused design, and ethical data presentation. 

---

## Project Objectives

This project seeks to answer the following questions:

- How have vehicle thefts changed across the United States in recent years?
- Which cities have experienced the highest levels of Kia and Hyundai thefts?
- How do theft trends vary by geographic region?
- Why have Kia and Hyundai vehicles become a significant target for theft?
- How can data visualization be used to communicate public safety concerns effectively?

---

## Dataset Sources

The analysis was conducted using publicly available vehicle theft datasets, including:

- Motherboard VICE News Kia/Hyundai Theft Data
- U.S. vehicle theft records aggregated by law enforcement agencies
- Geographic theft data used for regional and city-level analysis

The datasets contain vehicle theft counts, city-level theft records, regional trends, and historical theft activity from 2019 through 2023.

## Data Preparation

The datasets were cleaned and transformed prior to visualization development.

Key preparation steps included:

- Standardizing city and geographic identifiers
- Cleaning theft count variables
- Aggregating data by city, year, and region
- Calculating regional theft percentages
- Summarizing Kia and Hyundai theft totals
- Preparing data for infographic and visualization development

No records were deleted or altered beyond formatting corrections and aggregation procedures.

## Visualization Deliverables

### Public Awareness Infographic

The primary deliverable for this project was an infographic designed for the general public, particularly Kia and Hyundai owners living in areas affected by elevated vehicle theft rates.

#### Purpose

The infographic communicates the rapid increase in thefts while encouraging vehicle owners to take preventative security measures.

#### Design Principles

- Clear visual hierarchy
- Consistent color palette
- Minimal text
- Audience-focused messaging
- Action-oriented recommendations

The infographic was developed as part of a fictional public awareness campaign for SecureRide Technologies, a company providing vehicle security software and anti-theft tools. 

---

### Supporting Visualizations

Several supporting visualizations were created to explore theft patterns and identify key trends.

#### Top Cities for Kia & Hyundai Thefts

Analysis identified the cities with the highest reported Kia and Hyundai theft volumes between 2019 and 2023.

Key cities included:

- Chicago
- Milwaukee
- Los Angeles
- Denver
- Minneapolis

These locations experienced substantially higher theft volumes than most other metropolitan areas. 

---

### Regional Theft Analysis

Geographic visualizations examined how theft activity varied across different regions of the United States.

#### Key Findings

- The Western United States accounted for the largest share of overall vehicle thefts.
- The Midwest experienced the highest concentration of Kia and Hyundai thefts.
- Regional differences highlight how vehicle theft patterns vary significantly across the country.

---

### Milwaukee Case Study

A focused analysis of Milwaukee demonstrated the dramatic increase in Kia and Hyundai thefts beginning in 2021.

Visualizations showed that Kia and Hyundai thefts surged rapidly compared to other vehicle thefts during this period, illustrating why Milwaukee became one of the most frequently cited examples of the trend. 

---

## Key Findings

### Kia & Hyundai Thefts Increased Significantly

Several cities experienced sharp increases in theft activity beginning around 2021, coinciding with the emergence of widely publicized vehicle security vulnerabilities.

### Certain Cities Were Disproportionately Affected

Chicago, Milwaukee, Los Angeles, Denver, and Minneapolis recorded some of the highest totals for Kia and Hyundai thefts.

### The Midwest Was Most Impacted

More than half of the Kia and Hyundai thefts included in the analysis occurred within Midwestern cities, making it the most heavily affected region.

### Public Awareness Is Critical

The findings highlight the importance of educating vehicle owners about theft prevention strategies and available security technologies.

## Data Visualization Approach

This project focused on communicating public safety information through visual storytelling rather than predictive modeling.

Several visualization principles guided the design:

- Honest representation of data
- Consistent scales and formatting
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Audience-focused communication
- Ethical presentation of public data

The goal was to transform large volumes of theft data into visuals that could be quickly understood by a non-technical audience while preserving accuracy and transparency. 

---

## Lessons Learned

This project demonstrated how data visualization can be used to raise awareness about real-world public safety issues.

Key takeaways include:

- Visual storytelling can improve public understanding of complex issues.
- Geographic patterns become easier to identify through visualization.
- Infographics can communicate key findings more effectively than text alone.
- Ethical visualization practices are essential when presenting public safety data.
- Audience-centered design increases the impact of data-driven communication.
